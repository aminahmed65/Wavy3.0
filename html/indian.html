Indian Ocean

Overview
The Indian Ocean is the third largest ocean in the world and also holds 20% of the worlds total water, similar to the Atlantic Ocean. The Indian Ocean is approximately 80 million years old, it started to form about 140 million years ago yet all of the ocean’s basins are younger than 80 million years. The Indian Ocean connects Asia, Africa, and Australia making it a vital trading route for the world

History
The Indian Ocean was formed by two pre-historic oceans. These were the Tethys Ocean (South) and the Paleo-Tethys Ocean (North). The Indian Ocean was named that after the Ottoman Empire blocked the Silk route and there was a pursuit to establish a sea-route to India and China thus naming the ocean the Indian Ocean. But before that, the Ancient Indians called it the Mahasagara (the great sea of the Sindhu). A map of Africa dated 1747 depicts that the Indian Ocean was previously referred to as the Eastern Ocean. In all, people called the Indian ocean many different things through history.

Trade and Influence on Mankind
Studies show that 2.6 billion people (about 40% percent of the population) live in or around the Indian Ocean. This is why I think that the Indian Ocean s the network bridge to humanity. The Indian Ocean is such an important geographical wonder because it connects three different continents (Europe, Asia, Africa) allowing different forms of food, culture, and knowledge to be transported between each other. The Indian Ocean was a massive leap human because it facilitated growth as mankind. Without it, our lives (technologically) wouldn’t be as advanced as it is now.
