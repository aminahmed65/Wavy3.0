Atlantic Ocean

Overview
The Atlantic Ocean is the world’s second largest oceans just after the Pacific ocean. The ocean approximately covers about 20% of the world’s total area. The Atlantic oceans formed approximately 180 million years ago due to the splitting of tectonic plates. The Panthalassa Ocean (Pangea’s largest ocean) was split up into what we know now as the Atlantic Ocean, (East) Pacific Ocean (West) and the Arctic Ocean (North). The Atlantic Ocean is also categorized by the Northern Atlantic and the Southern Atlantic.

History
The name Atlantic was first used in Ancient Greece around 450 BC, Atlantic loosely translates to “the island of Atlas” or “sea of Atlas”. Atlas was the Greek Titan of navigation and astronomy, known for bearing the weight of the heavens on his shoulders, a punishment bestowed on him by Zeus. The oldest known mentions of an "Atlantic" sea come from Stesichorus around mid-sixth century BC.

Naming
The part of the Atlantic Ocean was also referred to as the Aethiopoan Ocean specifically the lower half near Africa. Also during the Age of Discovery English Cartographers called the Atlantic Ocean the Great Western Ocean. Furthermore, the Atlantic ocean is sometimes called “the pond” or the “great pond” typically used by British and American people, instances dating back as early as 1640.

Enviroment
Over 20,000 different species of fish and other aquatic animals are within the Atlantic Ocean. The aquatic wildlife within the Atlantic ocean is suffering from oil pollution and other forms of industrial waste and sewage in the Carribean Sea, the Gulf of Mexico and other Seas. An interesting fact is that Hurricanes are extremely prevalent in the western parts of the North Atlantic during the Summer and Fall but practically unknown in the South Atlantic.
